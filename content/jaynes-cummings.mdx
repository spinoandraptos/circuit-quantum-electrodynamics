# Jaynes–Cummings Model

The Jaynes–Cummings model describes a two-level system (qubit) coupled to a single
mode of the electromagnetic field. In circuit QED, the qubit is a superconducting
artificial atom and the field mode lives inside a microwave resonator.

## The Full Hamiltonian

The system Hamiltonian has three parts:

$$
\hat{H} = \hat{H}_\text{field} + \hat{H}_\text{qubit} + \hat{H}_\text{int}
$$

**Resonator (field mode)**:

$$
\hat{H}_\text{field} = \hbar\omega_r \left(\hat{a}^\dagger \hat{a} + \frac{1}{2}\right)
$$

where $\hat{a}^\dagger, \hat{a}$ are photon creation/annihilation operators with $[\hat{a}, \hat{a}^\dagger]=1$.

**Qubit**:

$$
\hat{H}_\text{qubit} = \frac{\hbar\omega_q}{2}\hat{\sigma}_z
$$

**Dipole interaction** (in the rotating-wave approximation):

$$
\hat{H}_\text{int} = \hbar g \left(\hat{a}^\dagger \hat{\sigma}_- + \hat{a}\,\hat{\sigma}_+\right)
$$

The coupling constant $g$ is set by the overlap of the vacuum electric field with the
qubit's dipole moment. The full **Jaynes–Cummings Hamiltonian** is:

$$
\boxed{\hat{H}_\text{JC} = \hbar\omega_r \hat{a}^\dagger\hat{a} + \frac{\hbar\omega_q}{2}\hat{\sigma}_z + \hbar g\!\left(\hat{a}^\dagger\hat{\sigma}_- + \hat{a}\,\hat{\sigma}_+\right)}
$$

## Dressed States (Eigenstates)

In the single-excitation subspace $\{|g,1\rangle,|e,0\rangle\}$, diagonalisation gives the **dressed states**:

$$
|\pm,n\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\!\left(|g,n{+}1\rangle \pm |e,n\rangle\right)
$$

with eigenvalues:

$$
E_{\pm,n} = \hbar\omega_r\!\left(n+\frac{1}{2}\right) \pm \frac{\hbar}{2}\sqrt{\Delta^2 + 4g^2(n+1)}
$$

where $\Delta = \omega_q - \omega_r$ is the **detuning**.

## Vacuum Rabi Splitting

On resonance ($\Delta = 0$), the two dressed-state energies split by $2\hbar g$. This is the
**vacuum Rabi splitting** — directly observable as two peaks in the cavity transmission spectrum.

# Input-Output Theory

Input-output theory describes how signals propagate into and out of a quantum system
coupled to external transmission lines. It is the theoretical backbone of microwave
readout in cQED.

## System-Bath Hamiltonian

Model the resonator as system $S$, coupled to a continuum of bath modes $\hat{b}_\omega$:

$$
\hat{H} = \hat{H}_S + \int_0^\infty d\omega\,\hbar\omega\,\hat{b}_\omega^\dagger\hat{b}_\omega
+ i\hbar\sqrt{\frac{\kappa}{2\pi}}\int_0^\infty d\omega\!\left(\hat{b}_\omega^\dagger\hat{a} - \hat{a}^\dagger\hat{b}_\omega\right)
$$

## Quantum Langevin Equation

In the Markov approximation, the intracavity field obeys:

$$
\dot{\hat{a}} = -\left(i\omega_r + \frac{\kappa}{2}\right)\hat{a} + \sqrt{\kappa}\,\hat{a}_\text{in}(t)
$$

where $\hat{a}_\text{in}$ is the input field operator satisfying $[\hat{a}_\text{in}(t), \hat{a}_\text{in}^\dagger(t')] = \delta(t-t')$.

## Input-Output Relation

The fundamental boundary condition:

$$
\boxed{\hat{a}_\text{out}(t) = \hat{a}_\text{in}(t) - \sqrt{\kappa}\,\hat{a}(t)}
$$

In the frequency domain, this gives the **transmission coefficient**:

$$
t(\omega) = \frac{\kappa}{\kappa/2 - i(\omega - \omega_r)}
$$

and the **reflection coefficient** (single-sided coupling):

$$
r(\omega) = \frac{\kappa/2 + i(\omega - \omega_r)}{\kappa/2 - i(\omega - \omega_r)}
$$

## Purcell Effect

When the qubit is coupled to the resonator, it acquires an additional decay channel via
the resonator's port — the **Purcell decay rate**:

$$
\gamma_P = \frac{g^2\kappa}{\Delta^2 + (\kappa/2)^2} \approx \frac{g^2}{\Delta^2}\kappa = \left(\frac{g}{\Delta}\right)^2\!\kappa
$$

In the dispersive limit this equals $\chi^2/\kappa$. Purcell filters — bandpass structures
at the cavity frequency — are used in practice to suppress $\gamma_P$ without sacrificing
readout bandwidth.

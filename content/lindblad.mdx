# Lindblad Master Equation

Open quantum systems — those coupled to an environment — cannot be described by a
pure-state Schrödinger equation. The **Lindblad (GKSL) master equation** gives the most
general Markovian evolution of the density matrix $\hat{\rho}$.

## The Master Equation

$$
\boxed{\dot{\hat{\rho}} = -\frac{i}{\hbar}[\hat{H}, \hat{\rho}] + \sum_k \gamma_k \mathcal{D}[\hat{L}_k]\hat{\rho}}
$$

where the **Lindblad dissipator** acts as:

$$
\mathcal{D}[\hat{L}]\hat{\rho} = \hat{L}\hat{\rho}\hat{L}^\dagger - \frac{1}{2}\hat{L}^\dagger\hat{L}\hat{\rho} - \frac{1}{2}\hat{\rho}\hat{L}^\dagger\hat{L}
$$

## Jump Operators in cQED

| Process | Jump operator $\hat{L}_k$ | Rate $\gamma_k$ |
|---|---|---|
| Qubit relaxation ($T_1$) | $\hat{\sigma}_-$ | $\gamma_1 = 1/T_1$ |
| Pure dephasing | $\hat{\sigma}_z/2$ | $\gamma_\phi$ |
| Resonator decay | $\hat{a}$ | $\kappa$ |
| Thermal excitation | $\hat{\sigma}_+$ | $\gamma_1 \bar{n}_\text{th}$ |

## Bloch Equation Derivation

Tracing out the resonator and applying the Lindblad equation in the qubit subspace yields
the **optical Bloch equations** for $\langle\hat{\sigma}_\pm\rangle$ and $\langle\hat{\sigma}_z\rangle$:

$$
\frac{d\langle\hat{\sigma}_-\rangle}{dt} = -\frac{1}{T_2}\langle\hat{\sigma}_-\rangle - i\frac{\Omega}{2}\langle\hat{\sigma}_z\rangle
$$

$$
\frac{d\langle\hat{\sigma}_z\rangle}{dt} = -\frac{\langle\hat{\sigma}_z\rangle - \langle\hat{\sigma}_z\rangle_\text{eq}}{T_1} + i\Omega(\langle\hat{\sigma}_-\rangle - \langle\hat{\sigma}_+\rangle)
$$

## Coherence Times

The two fundamental decoherence times are:

$$
\frac{1}{T_1} = \gamma_1 = \Gamma_\downarrow + \Gamma_\uparrow
$$

$$
\frac{1}{T_2} = \frac{1}{2T_1} + \frac{1}{T_\phi}
$$

where $T_\phi = 1/\gamma_\phi$ is the **pure dephasing time**. Note $T_2 \leq 2T_1$ always,
with equality when pure dephasing is absent. In state-of-the-art transmons,
$T_1, T_2 \sim 100\,\mu\text{s}$.

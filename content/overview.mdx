# Circuit Quantum Electrodynamics

**Circuit QED (cQED)** is the microwave analogue of cavity QED: a superconducting qubit plays
the role of an artificial atom, and a microwave resonator (coplanar waveguide or 3D cavity)
plays the role of the optical cavity. Fabricated on a chip, cQED systems are engineered to
reach the **strong coupling regime** where the coherent qubit–photon interaction rate $g$
exceeds all decoherence rates.

## Why cQED?

| Parameter | Optical cavity QED | Circuit QED |
|---|---|---|
| Coupling $g/2\pi$ | ~10 MHz | ~100–500 MHz |
| $g/\kappa$ | 1–10 | 10–1000 |
| Temperature | Room / mK (ions) | ~20 mK |
| Scalability | Low | High (chip-scale) |

The large coupling arises because the vacuum electric field in a superconducting resonator can be
enormous ($\sim 0.1\,\text{V/m}$) and the artificial-atom dipole moment is engineered to be large.

## Key Energy Scales

The physics is governed by four energy scales:

$$
\omega_r,\quad \omega_q,\quad g,\quad \kappa,\quad \gamma
$$

where $\omega_r$ ($\omega_q$) is the resonator (qubit) angular frequency, $g$ the vacuum Rabi
coupling, $\kappa$ the resonator decay rate, and $\gamma$ the qubit relaxation rate. The
hierarchy $g \gg \kappa, \gamma$ defines strong coupling.

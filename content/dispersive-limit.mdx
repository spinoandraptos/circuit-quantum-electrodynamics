# Dispersive Limit

When the qubit–resonator detuning $\Delta = \omega_q - \omega_r$ is large compared to the
coupling $g$ (i.e. $|g/\Delta| \ll 1$), the system enters the **dispersive regime**.
Direct photon–qubit exchange is suppressed; instead, each subsystem acquires a small
frequency shift that depends on the state of the other.

## Dispersive Transformation

Apply the unitary transformation:

$$
\hat{U} = \exp\!\left[\frac{g}{\Delta}\!\left(\hat{a}^\dagger\hat{\sigma}_- - \hat{a}\,\hat{\sigma}_+\right)\right]
$$

Expanding to second order in $\lambda = g/\Delta$:

$$
\hat{H}_\text{disp} = \hat{U}\hat{H}_\text{JC}\hat{U}^\dagger \approx \hbar\tilde{\omega}_r\hat{a}^\dagger\hat{a} + \frac{\hbar\tilde{\omega}_q}{2}\hat{\sigma}_z + \hbar\chi\,\hat{a}^\dagger\hat{a}\,\hat{\sigma}_z
$$

## The Dispersive Hamiltonian

$$
\boxed{\hat{H}_\text{disp} = \hbar\!\left(\omega_r + \chi\hat{\sigma}_z\right)\!\hat{a}^\dagger\hat{a} + \frac{\hbar\tilde{\omega}_q}{2}\hat{\sigma}_z}
$$

The **dispersive shift** is:

$$
\chi = \frac{g^2}{\Delta}
$$

(For the transmon, the full expression also includes a correction from higher levels.)

## Physical Interpretation

The resonator frequency shifts by $\pm\chi$ depending on the qubit state:

$$
\omega_r^{(g)} = \omega_r - \chi, \qquad \omega_r^{(e)} = \omega_r + \chi
$$

This **qubit-state-dependent pull** of the resonator is the basis of **dispersive readout**:
a microwave probe tone reveals the qubit state via the phase of the reflected signal,
without absorbing a real photon — a QND (quantum non-demolition) measurement.

## Validity Condition

The dispersive approximation breaks down when the photon number $\bar{n}$ approaches the
**critical photon number**:

$$
n_\text{crit} = \frac{\Delta^2}{4g^2}
$$

Above $n_\text{crit}$ the system re-enters the resonant regime and the qubit is dressed again.
